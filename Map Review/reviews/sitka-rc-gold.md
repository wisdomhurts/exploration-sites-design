## Detailed Recommendations

### Text & Spelling
- **"Snowline's Valley Deposit"** → likely "Valley Deposit" or "Valley Deposit (Snowline)". No other deposit uses possessive form
- **"200km"** → "200 km" (add space per SI convention)
- **NWT / BC** as abbreviations while ALASKA / YUKON are spelled out — standardize (either all spelled out or all abbreviated)
- **MAYO** appears bolder than other towns — unclear why. Standardize or document in legend if intentional
- **Add:** title, figure number, date, source attribution, projection statement

### Color
- **Ocean:** #173559 (Atmosphere Blue) at 85%, or #9ECDE4 (Sky Blue) — not gray
- **Tombstone Gold Belt:** #C8A96A (Gold Refined) at 25-30% with 1px #C8A96A boundary stroke
- **Tintina Gold Province:** #C4572A (Gold Highlight) at 20-25% with 1px #C4572A boundary stroke
- **Deposit markers:** Standardize to #173559 (Atmosphere Blue) filled circles, 8-10px. Reserve orange exclusively for RC Gold
- **Fort Knox:** Use same deposit marker style, not a unique orange oval
- **Inset globe:** Ocean #173559, land #EDEDED, marker #C4572A
- **Compass rose:** #040404, simplified to minimal north arrow

### Layout
- Remove duplicate logo — one placement only
- Replace generic callout box with clean title block (no ornamental border)
- Add claim boundary outline for RC Gold property
- Stagger right-side deposit labels to prevent crowding
- Scale bar: add subdivisions (0/50/100/200 km), match style to compass rose

### Symbology
- RC Gold marker: 1.5-2x larger than deposit markers — dominant focal point
- Fort Knox: standardize to same "Major Gold Deposit" symbol, or add unique symbol to legend
- Town dots: add to legend ("Town / City")
- Political boundary dashes: verify distinct from "Regional Structure" dashes
- Legend: group entries under subheadings (Project, Geology, Infrastructure)

## Art Direction

The geology on this map is doing the work — the Tombstone Gold Belt diagonal and the cluster of proven deposits tell exactly the story an investor needs to hear. What holds it back is the packaging. Strip every decorative element — the ornamental callout box, the repeated logo, the fancy compass rose — and replace with a clean title block, a single logo, and a simple north arrow. Make the RC Gold marker the unmistakable focal point at 2x the size of neighboring deposits. Add a claim boundary so investors can see the land position. Lock the geological zones to distinct, controlled palette colors. The content is premium — the presentation needs to match it.
