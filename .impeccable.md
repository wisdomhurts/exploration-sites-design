## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** CEOs, VPs of IR, and geologists at junior mining and exploration-stage resource companies (TSXV, CSE, ASX-listed). They discover ES through referrals, conferences (PDAC, Roundup, AME), or web searches. They browse on desktop during market hours for research and on mobile at events. Secondary audience: institutional fund managers who encounter ES deliverables (maps, decks, websites) and trace them back to the agency.

### Brand Personality
**3 words:** Authoritative. Understated. Meticulous.

**Emotional target:** Quiet authority within 5 seconds. "These people are serious. This isn't a typical agency." The site should feel like walking into a well-appointed office — nothing screams, but every detail signals competence.

**Tone:** Confident, precise, quietly expert. Think *The Economist* meets a senior exploration geologist meets a fund's quarterly letter. Numbers over adjectives. Active voice. No hype words.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual reference:** Pentagram / Sagmeister — portfolio-forward, let the work do the talking. Editorial precision in typography and layout, but the craft IS the primary visual element.

**Theme:** Light mode. Warm paper tones (Quartz #F4F1EC), not cold white. The palette should feel excavated — deep neutrals, cartographic mid-tones, a single restrained gold accent (Ore #B8823A) at ≤2%.

**Anti-references:** SaaS landing pages. Dark mode with neon accents. Anything that reads as "agency-slick" or "startup-y." No glassmorphism, no gradient text, no bouncy animations.

**Typography:** Three voices, never a fourth. Editorial serif (GT Sectra Display / Source Serif 4) for headlines. Technical sans (Söhne / Inter) for body. Precision mono (Söhne Mono / JetBrains Mono) for data only.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint is the luxury.** Subtract before adding. White space is the most expensive element.
2. **Evidence before assertions.** Every claim has a source. Every data point is defensible. The site must survive a skeptical portfolio manager.
3. **The work is the proof.** Portfolio and case studies do more than copy. Visual deliverables should be visible, not described.
4. **Sector-native, not outsider polish.** The design should feel like it comes from someone who knows strike, dip, and grade — not someone who Googled "mining company" last Tuesday.
5. **Institutional register, editorial craft.** The intersection of a fund's annual report and a Pentagram case study.

### Accessibility
Pragmatic minimum — good contrast, readable text, functional on all devices. Focus on usability over certification. Keyboard navigation for interactive elements. No reduced-motion violations.
